Concept Work / Research and Design


Ride the coattails of engineers with wires orchestrated by conductors in this audio texture engine synthesizer.

Conductor's role in an orchestra

coordinating sections
attenuating performance
Cuing key events

Electrical conductive properties of materials

as Resistance increases, Current decreases
Current can be interpretted as a modulation source
or more specifically as the frequency of a modulation source

as Ampacity increases, Temperature increases
Temperature can be interpretted as the frequency distribution of partials or as the color of the sound
such that the spectral distribution increases into the higher frequency ranges as Temperature increases
As a maximum Temperature is approached, the timbre of the sound can change
and inversely, as the minimum Temperature is approached the timbre of the sound can change
such that the sound has a differnt character at the extremes of its Temperature ranges than it does within a normal range
There will need to be some way to determine the exact maximum and minimum Temperature
as well as the method of timbral modulation and distribution of modulation ammount before extreme ranges 

I should introduce a model to organize all of these relationships into a single Concept
Let the model be a wire with properties for:
 - material (Ressistivity): affects frenquency of modulation source
 - material (Temperature): affects timbral distortion distribution, maximum and minimum values
 - wire diameter (Ampacity): affects the amplitude of modulation source

Ampacity ==> modulation Amplitude
Ressistivity ==> modulation Frequency

Perhaps, the wire can be thought of as a carrier of total power ammount, where that power distributes between properties.
In this idea, the wire model essentially determines how to distribute the power sent through it amongst its wire properties.

If wire properties include wire length, I can start to think of the timing of the power as it is sent through the wire.
This would allow me to consider the harmonics between power transmissions in multiple wires,
which would help to bridge the conceptual gap between orchestral conductors and material conductors,
because the harmonics can be managed by some model for a conductor in the orchestral sense.

Wire Model
Material (physicochemical properties of material)
 - Thermochromism: ammount of change in frequency distribution, ammount of Thermistor Effect (decreases Resistance as temperature increases)
 - Thermal expansion and contraction: timbral deformation type and curvature of deformation ammount around temperature extremes
 - Ressistivity: material maximum (melting point) and minimum (freezing point) Temperature, maximum propagation speed
 - Ductility: ammount by which a wire of this material can be attenuated by the Conductor Model (by which Diamter can be reduced to increase Length)
Geometry (phyical characteristics of wire)
 - Diameter: Increases Ampacity and Ressistivity as this characteristic increases
 - Length: multiplies by Resistance to determine Velocity

 - Velocity: calculated propagation speed
 - Frequency: Velocity over period (treat wire Length as wave period to prevent Gibbs phenomenon)

Conductor Model
Comodualtion (combines modulation sources--properties from wire models--into a single comodulation source)
 - Frequency Harmonization: attenuate wire Geometry to bring  (ductility: increase length => decrease diameter)
 - Ampacity Normalization: let the comodulation source amplitude be the median of the wires' ampacity
 - Bandwidth Smoothing: set a fixed maximum ammount of change in comodulation source properties per unit time
Cuing: modulaton sources only run and Comodulation source calculation only occurs while cuing (playing a note)

Under the hood
3 Wire models for modulation source properties
1 conductor model for comodulation source properties
3 polyphonic generators (sections) that carry the comodulation source

Over the hood
For each Wire,
 - Choose a material to display a material/transmission property table
 - tweak geometric parameters to affect values in table to taste
For the Conductor,
 - control comodulation for a more or less orchestrated modulation
 - control ammount of modulation (comodulation amplitude)
 - Select gate shape (ramp down, ramp up, triangle)
 - Select gate ammount (percent ammount of comodulation amplitude)
 - Select gate speed (1:1, 1:2, 1:3, 1:4 ratio between rhythm period and comodulation period)
For each Section,
 - Select a fourier series waveform (triangle, square, or saw) from a list to generate that kind of waveform for voices in section
 - Select number of voices in section (monophonic to duophonic to polyphonic; 1, 2 or 3 voices)
 - Select ammount of phase shift between voices (unison knob)
 - note: let each section have its own running phase to prevent unison between sections
 - Adjust intensity parameter (increase voice amplitude and filter cutoff and decrease filter resonance) to affect the relative intensity of that section to others

conditional tree is good for short FX chains
but does not scale for longer FX chain
what if the effects each had a chain number, which could be used to determine their place in the overall chain
This approach, however, would require user input validation to ensure there aren't duplicate chain numbers
alternatively, I could have FX elements in an order within the GUI and a control for changing order
and just increase the chain number by one for each next element in the order,
but the DOM is so subject to mutation that storing data, like the order of FX in FX chain, in the GUI is not reliable.
Before trying to solve the problem of how to control the order of FX in the FX chain, it may be useful to first create effects
and then use those effects as a basis for considering how differnt chain orders would world work.
